📘 BUSINESS REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT (BRD)
Product: Cohi Workbench

1. Executive Summary

Cohi Workbench is an AI-powered intelligence workspace within the Coheus platform designed to enable lenders to explore, refine, and present their business data with confidence. It allows users to leverage existing Coheus dashboards, curated Cohi Dashboard templates, and conversational AI guidance to create executive-ready reports, dashboards, and presentations without dependency on analysts or BI teams.

Cohi operates as the user’s personal senior analyst and data scientist, providing intelligence, guidance, and context to ensure insights are accurate, relevant, and suitable for executive oversight, board meetings, and committee reviews.

2. Business Objectives

The primary objectives of Cohi Workbench are to:

Enable self-service analytics while maintaining governance and consistency

Allow lenders to easily update and adapt existing Coheus visualizations

Accelerate preparation of executive, board, and committee materials

Standardize best-practice analytics through curated Cohi Dashboards

Reduce reliance on analysts for recurring reporting and presentations

Increase adoption and daily engagement with the Coheus platform

Ensure insights are explainable, trustworthy, and decision-oriented

3. Business Problem Statement

Lenders often face challenges in preparing timely, accurate, and executive-ready insights due to:

Static dashboards that are difficult to adapt

Heavy reliance on analysts for custom reporting

Last-minute rework for board and committee presentations

Inconsistent interpretations of performance data

Tools that are powerful but overly complex

Cohi Workbench addresses these challenges by providing a guided, AI-assisted workspace that balances flexibility with simplicity and governance.

8. Cohi Workbench Concept & Capabilities
   8.1 Workbench as an Intelligence Workspace

Cohi Workbench is not a traditional BI editor. It is a guided intelligence environment where users can:

Ask questions in natural language

Start from trusted dashboards instead of blank canvases

Modify existing visualizations safely and confidently

Receive AI guidance on what to show and why

8.2 Use of Existing Coheus Visualizations

Lenders must be able to:

Access existing Coheus dashboards

Copy dashboards into their personal workspace

Modify measures, dimensions, dates, and visualizations

Preserve the integrity of system defaults while allowing flexibility

Users are always informed when they are working on a copy, not a system-controlled view.

9. Cohi Dashboard Library (Templates)
   9.1 Business Requirement

The platform shall provide a Cohi Dashboard Library consisting of curated, lender-proven dashboards developed and maintained by Coheus.

These dashboards serve as:

Best-practice benchmarks

Standardized starting points

AI-enabled templates

9.2 Business Capabilities

Users must be able to:

Browse dashboards by category and use case

Preview dashboards prior to use

Copy dashboards into their workspace or folders

Ask Cohi to use a dashboard as a starting point

9.3 Governance

Master dashboards are read-only

User copies are fully editable

Updates to master dashboards do not overwrite user copies

Users may be notified when newer versions are available

10. Role of Cohi (AI Intelligence)
    10.1 Cohi as Senior Analyst & Data Scientist

Cohi must behave as a trusted internal expert who:

Understands lending and financial performance

Interprets business intent, not just requests

Recommends appropriate views for the audience

Explains trends, risks, and implications

10.2 Guidance for Oversight & Governance

Cohi actively supports preparation for:

Executive reviews

Board meetings

Credit, risk, and operations committees

Examples of guidance include:

Recommending summary views over operational detail

Highlighting trends that require explanation

Suggesting executive narratives and talking points

Proposing board-ready slide structures

11. UI/UX Experience Requirements
    11.1 Design Mandate

The Workbench UI/UX must be:

Clean – minimal clutter, focused on outcomes

Simple – no training required

Intuitive – clear next steps at all times

The interface must never feel like a technical BI tool.

11.2 Interaction Model

Natural language interaction is primary

Configuration is progressive and optional

Advanced controls are AI-assisted

Undo and revert are always available

11.3 Executive-Ready by Default

All dashboards and reports must:

Use consistent, professional formatting

Be suitable for leadership consumption

Include AI-generated summaries where appropriate

Support an “Executive View” mode

12. Presentation & Board Readiness
    12.1 Business Requirement

Outputs created in Cohi Workbench must be ready for:

Executive oversight

Board presentations

Committee discussions

Without requiring rework in external tools.

12.2 Export Expectations

Users must be able to export:

PowerPoint (board-ready slides)

Word (narrative reports)

PDF

Excel (underlying data support)

13. Sharing & Collaboration

Users can share individual dashboards, reports, or entire folders

Sharing supports individuals and groups

Permissions include view, comment, and edit

Optional expiration dates enforce governance

14. Business Rules & Governance

Role-based access control enforced at all times

No cross-tenant data exposure

Shared assets inherit the lowest permission level

AI outputs must be explainable and auditable

All major actions are logged for audit purposes

15. Business Outcome & North Star

The success of Cohi Workbench is achieved when:

Lenders can confidently walk into executive, board, or committee meetings knowing that the insights, visuals, and narratives presented were intelligently prepared, clearly communicated, and contextually accurate — without analyst bottlenecks or last-minute rework.
